The Opportunity
The AEC Industry Has
No Central Platform
While every industry has its professional network, the $13 trillion built environment operates on fragmented tools, word-of-mouth referrals, and outdated directories.

Hiring is Broken

Firms rely on recruiters, personal networks, and generic job boards that don't understand AEC specializations. Finding a licensed architect with Revit expertise and healthcare experience takes weeks.

No Professional Network

LinkedIn serves every industry poorly rather than one industry well. AEC professionals need portfolio-driven profiles, project-based credentials, and industry-specific discovery tools.

Product Spec is Analog

Architects specify $500B+ in building products annually using PDFs, binder samples, and trade shows. No platform connects manufacturers directly to the architects making specification decisions.

Freelance is Invisible

43% of AEC work involves freelancers and consultants, yet there's no marketplace for project-based architecture and engineering talent. Billions in work flows through personal referrals alone.

A SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is the most common instrument for early-stage investment. Created by Y Combinator, it lets you invest today and receive equity later — when the company raises a priced round.
